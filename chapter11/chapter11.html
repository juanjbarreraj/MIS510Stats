Exercise 11-018 Algo (Testing the Population Mean When the Population Standard Deviation is Known)

Calculate the p-value of the test to determine that there is sufficient evidence to infer the research objective.

Research objective: The population mean is not equal to 0.

Given: σ = 60, n = 90, x̄ = −5.2

Identify the hypotheses.

  • H0: μ = 0
  • H1: μ ≠ 0

Calculate the value of the test statistic.

z = -0.82

Determine the p-value.

p-value = 0.4122

Interpret the result.

There is not enough evidence to reject the null hypothesis.

Exercise 11-036 Algo (Testing the Population Mean When the Population Standard Deviation is Known)

A business student claims that, on average, an MBA student is required to prepare more than five cases per week. To examine the claim, a statistics professor asks a random sample of 10 MBA students to report the number of cases they prepare weekly. The results are exhibited here.

1 7 4 8 9 5 12 3 7 4

Can the professor conclude at the 5% significance level that the claim is true, assuming that the number of cases is normally distributed with a standard deviation of 1.7?

State the hypotheses.

  • H0: μ = 5
  • H1: μ > 5

Calculate the test statistic.

1.86

Find the p-value.

0.0314

State your conclusion.

There is enough evidence to infer that an MBA student is required to prepare more than five cases per week.

Exercise 11-077 Algo (Calculating the Probability of a Type II Error)

A school-board administrator believes that the average number of days absent per year among students is less than 9 days and that the population standard deviation is 4 days. In testing to determine whether this belief is true, the administrator could use one of the following plans.

Find the probability of a Type II error for each plan, given that the true population average is 8 days.

(a) n = 100, α = 0.01

0.4325

(b) n = 85, α = 0.05

0.2546

(c) n = 40, α = 0.10

0.3821

Which plan has the lowest probability of a Type II error, given that the true population average is 8 days?

Plan (b) has the lowest probability of a Type II error if the true population average is 8 days.
