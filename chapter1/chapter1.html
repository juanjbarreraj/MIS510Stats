Exercise 01-001 (Key Statistical Concepts)

In your own words, define and give an example of each of the following statistical terms.

(a) Population

Correct Answer: The whole group you are interested in. Example: Everyone who goes to a gym in your city.

(b) Sample

Correct Answer: A smaller group taken from the population. Example: 50 people from that gym who you ask about their workout habits.

(c) Parameter

Correct Answer: A number that describes the whole group. Example: The real average number of hours all gym members train per week.

(d) Statistic

Correct Answer: A number based only on the sample. Example: The average hours trained per week by the 50 people you asked.

(e) Statistical Inference

Correct Answer: Using the small group to make a guess about the whole group. Example: Using the 50 people’s answers to estimate how much all gym members train.

Exercise 01-002 (Key Statistical Concepts)

Briefly describe the difference between descriptive statistics and inferential statistics.

  1. Descriptive statistics is a body of methods used to draw conclusions about characteristics of populations based on sample data. Inferential statistics deals with methods of organizing, summarizing, and presenting data in a convenient and informative way.
  2. Descriptive statistics deals with methods of organizing, summarizing, and presenting data in a convenient and informative way. Inferential statistics is a body of methods used to draw conclusions about characteristics of populations based on sample data.
  3. Descriptive statistics can only be performed once inferential statistics has been completed.
  4. There is no difference between descriptive and inferential statistics.

Correct Answer: 2. Descriptive statistics deals with methods of organizing, summarizing, and presenting data in a convenient and informative way. Inferential statistics is a body of methods used to draw conclusions about characteristics of populations based on sample data.

Exercise 01-003 (Key Statistical Concepts)

A politician who is running for the office of mayor of a city with 29,000 registered voters commissions a survey. In the survey, 43% of the 300 registered voters interviewed say they plan to vote for the politician.

(a) What is the population of interest?

Correct Answer: 29,000 registered voters

(b) What is the sample?

Correct Answer: 300 registered voters

(c) Is the value 43% a parameter or a statistic? Explain.

Correct Answer: Statistic

Exercise 01-004 (Key Statistical Concepts)

A manufacturer of computer chips claims that less than 8% of its products are defective. When 1,400 chips were drawn from a large production, 5% were found to be defective.

(a) What is the population of interest?

Correct Answer: Complete production run

(b) What is the sample?

Correct Answer: 1,400 chips

(c) What is the parameter?

Correct Answer: Proportion of defective chips in the production run

(d) What is the statistic?

Correct Answer: Proportion of defective chips in the sample

(e) Does the value 8% refer to the parameter or to the statistic?

Correct Answer: Parameter

(f) Is the value 5% a parameter or a statistic?

Correct Answer: Statistic

(g) Explain briefly how the statistic can be used to make inferences about the parameter to test the claim.

Correct Answer: We can use the sample statistic to estimate the population parameter.

Exercise 01-006 (Key Statistical Concepts)

You are shown a coin that its owner says is fair in the sense that it will produce the same number of heads and tails when flipped 100 times.

(a) Which of the following experiments can you use to test this claim?

  1. Weigh the coin. If it weighs the same with heads up versus tails up, it is fair.
  2. Flip the coin twice. If it is fair, one flip will be heads and the other tails.
  3. Flip the coin 100 times and record the number of heads and tails.
  4. Use the coin for a purchase. If it is accepted by the cashier, it is fair.

Correct Answer: 3. Flip the coin 100 times and record the number of heads and tails.

(b) What is the population in your experiment?

  1. The number of “heads” and “tails” after flipping the coin 100 times.
  2. The proportion of heads or tails in the population.
  3. The proportion of heads or tails in the sample.
  4. The theoretical result of flipping the coin an infinite number of times and recording either “heads” or “tails.”

Correct Answer: 4. The theoretical result of flipping the coin an infinite number of times and recording either “heads” or “tails.”

(c) What is the sample?

  1. The number of “heads” and “tails” after flipping the coin 100 times.
  2. The proportion of heads or tails in the population.
  3. The proportion of heads or tails in the sample.
  4. The theoretical result of flipping the coin an infinite number of times and recording either “heads” or “tails.”

Correct Answer: 1. The number of “heads” and “tails” after flipping the coin 100 times.

(d) What is the parameter?

  1. The number of “heads” and “tails” after flipping the coin 100 times.
  2. The proportion of heads or tails in the population.
  3. The proportion of heads or tails in the sample.
  4. The theoretical result of flipping the coin an infinite number of times and recording either “heads” or “tails.”

Correct Answer: 2. The proportion of heads or tails in the population.

(e) What is the statistic?

  1. The number of “heads” and “tails” after flipping the coin 100 times.
  2. The proportion of heads or tails in the population.
  3. The proportion of heads or tails in the sample.
  4. The theoretical result of flipping the coin an infinite number of times and recording either “heads” or “tails.”

Correct Answer: 3. The proportion of heads or tails in the sample.

(f) Describe briefly how statistical inference can be used to test the claim.

Correct Answer: The sample statistic can be used to test the claim that the coin is fair.

Exercise 01-007 (Key Statistical Concepts)

You are shown a coin that its owner says is fair in the sense that it will produce the same number of heads and tails when flipped a very large number of times. Suppose that you decide to flip the coin 100 times.

(a) What conclusion would you be likely to draw if you observed 98 heads?

Correct Answer: The coin is not fair.

(b) What conclusion would you be likely to draw if you observed 59 heads?

Correct Answer: The coin may or may not be fair.

(c) If you flip a perfectly fair coin 100 times, will you always observe exactly 50 heads?

Correct Answer: No

Exercise 01-008 (Key Statistical Concepts)

The owner of a large fleet of taxis is trying to estimate costs for next year’s operations. One major cost is fuel purchase. To estimate fuel purchase, the owner needs to know the total distance his taxis will travel next year, the cost of a gallon of fuel, and the fuel mileage of his taxis. The owner has been provided with the first two figures (distance estimate and cost of a gallon of fuel). However, because of the high cost of gasoline, the owner has converted the fleet’s taxis to operate on propane. The propane mileage (in miles per gallon) for 50 taxis has been measured and recorded.

(a) What is the population of interest?

Correct Answer: Propane mileage of all the cars in the fleet

(b) What is the parameter the owner needs?

Correct Answer: Mean propane mileage of all the cars in the fleet

(c) What is the sample?

Correct Answer: Propane mileage of the 50 cars

(d) What is the statistic?

Correct Answer: Mean propane mileage of the 50 cars

(e) Describe briefly how the statistic will produce the kind of information the owner wants.

Correct Answer: We can use the sample statistic to estimate the population parameter.
