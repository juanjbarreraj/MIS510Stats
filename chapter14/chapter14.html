Exercise 14-103 (Randomized Block Two-Way Analysis of Variance)

A recruiter for a computer company would like to determine whether there are differences in sales ability between business, arts, and science graduates. The recruiter takes a random sample of 20 business graduates who have been working for the company for the past 2 years. Each is then matched with an arts graduate and a science graduate with similar educational and working experience. The commission earned by each (in $1,000s) in the last year was recorded.

(a) Is there sufficient evidence to allow the recruiter to conclude that there are differences in sales ability between the holders of the three types of degrees?

  1. H0: μ1 = μ2 = μ3. H1: At least two means differ.
  2. H0: μ1 ≠ μ2 ≠ μ3. H1: At least two means are the same.
  3. H0: μ1 ≠ μ2 ≠ … ≠ μ20. H1: At least two means are the same.
  4. H0: μ1 = μ2 = … = μ20. H1: At least two means differ.

Correct Answer: i

Test statistic: 0.86

p-value: 0.4313

There is not enough evidence to conclude that there are differences in sales ability between the holders of the three degrees.

(b) Conduct a test to determine whether an independent samples design would have been a better choice.

  1. H0: μ1 = μ2 = μ3. H1: At least two means differ.
  2. H0: μ1 ≠ μ2 ≠ μ3. H1: At least two means are the same.
  3. H0: μ1 ≠ μ2 ≠ … ≠ μ20. H1: At least two means are the same.
  4. H0: μ1 = μ2 = … = μ20. H1: At least two means differ.

Correct Answer: iv

Test statistic: 26.64

p-value: 0.0000

There is enough evidence to indicate that there are differences between the blocks of students. The independent samples design would not be recommended.

(c) What are the required conditions for the test in part (a)?

The commissions for each type of degree are required to be normally distributed with the same variance.

(d) Are the required conditions satisfied?

  1. No. The condition regarding the variances appears to be violated since the largest treatment variance is over two times the lowest.
  2. No. The condition regarding the distribution of the data appears to be violated since only the histogram for science graduates appears sufficiently skewed. The other two histograms, especially the one for business graduates, are not especially skewed.
  3. No. The condition regarding the distribution of the data appears to be violated since only the histogram for arts graduates appears sufficiently uniform. The other two histograms, especially the one for business graduates, are not especially uniform.
  4. No. The condition regarding the distribution of the data appears to be violated since only the histogram for business graduates appears sufficiently bell-shaped. The other two histograms, especially the one for science graduates, are not especially bell-shaped.
  5. No. The condition regarding the variances appears to be violated since the largest treatment variance is less than two times the lowest.
