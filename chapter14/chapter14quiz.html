Exercise 14-006 Algo (One-Way Analysis of Variance)

Many college and university students obtain summer jobs. A statistics professor wanted to determine whether students in different degree programs earn different amounts. A random sample of 5 students in the B.A., B.Sc., and B.B.A. programs were asked to report what they earned the previous summer. The results (in $1,000s) are listed here.

B.A. B.Sc. B.B.A.
3.44.04.1
2.65.06.3
4.54.06.2
5.36.36.0
3.84.76.3

Can the professor infer at the 5% significance level that students in different degree programs differ in their summer earnings?

State the null and alternative hypotheses.

  1. H0: At least two means differ. H1: μ1 ≠ μ2 ≠ μ3
  2. H0: μ1 = μ2 = μ3. H1: At least two means differ.
  3. H0: μ1 ≠ μ2 ≠ μ3. H1: At least two means are the same.
  4. H0: At least two means differ. H1: μ1 = μ2 = μ3

Correct Answer: ii

Test statistic: 4.54

p-value: 0.0340

There is enough evidence to conclude that students in different degree programs differ in their summer earnings.

Exercise 14-093 Algo (Randomized Block (Two-Way) Analysis of Variance)

The following statistics were generated from a randomized block experiment with k = 3 and b = 7:

  • SST = 110
  • SSB = 46
  • SSE = 25

(a) Test to determine whether the treatment means differ. (Use α = 0.05.)

State the null and alternative hypotheses.

  1. H0: μ[T1] ≠ μ[T2] ≠ … ≠ μ[T7] ; H1: At least two treatment means are the same.
  2. H0: μ[T1] = μ[T2] = … = μ[T3] ; H1: At least two treatment means differ.
  3. H0: μ[T1] = μ[T2] = … = μ[T7] ; H1: At least two treatment means differ.
  4. H0: μ[T1] ≠ μ[T2] ≠ μ[T3] ; H1: At least two treatment means are the same.

Correct Answer: ii

Test statistic: 26.4

p-value: 0.0000

There is enough evidence to conclude that the treatment means differ.

  2. H0: μ[B1] = μ[B2] = μ[B3] ; H1: At least two block means differ.
  3. H0: μ[B1] ≠ μ[B2] ≠ μ[B3] ; H1: At least two block means are the same.
  4. H0: μ[B1] ≠ μ[B2] ≠ … ≠ μ[B7] ; H1: At least two block means are the same.

Exercise 14-109 Algo (Two-Factor Analysis of Variance)

A statistics practitioner conducted a two-factor analysis of variance experiment with a = 4, b = 3, and r = 8. The sums of squares are listed here:

  • SS(Total) = 9,490
  • SS(A) = 263
  • SS(B) = 829
  • SS(AB) = 543

(a) Test at the 5% significance level to determine whether factors A and B interact.

State the null and alternative hypotheses.

  1. H0: Factors A and B do interact to affect the mean responses. H1: Factors A and B do not interact to affect the mean responses.
  2. H0: Factors A and B show weak interaction. H1: Factors A and B show strong interaction.
  3. H0: Factors A and B do not interact to affect the mean responses. H1: Factors A and B do interact to affect the mean responses.
  4. H0: Factors A and B show strong interaction. H1: Factors A and B show weak interaction.

Correct Answer: iii

Test statistic: 0.97

p-value: 0.4521

There is not enough evidence to conclude that factors A and B interact.

(b) Test at the 5% significance level to determine whether differences exist between the levels of factor A.

State the null and alternative hypotheses.

  1. H0: The means of the levels of factor A are all different. H1: At least two means are the same.
  2. H0: The means of the levels of factor A are equal. H1: At least two means differ.
  3. H0: At least two means are the same. H1: The means of the levels of factor A are all different.
  4. H0: At least two means differ. H1: The means of the levels of factor A are equal.

Correct Answer: ii

Test statistic: 0.94

p-value: 0.4263

There is not enough evidence to conclude that differences exist between the levels of factor A.

(c) Test at the 5% significance level to determine whether differences exist between the levels of factor B.

State the null and alternative hypotheses.

  1. H0: The means of the levels of factor B are all different. H1: At least two means are the same.
  2. H0: The means of the levels of factor B are equal. H1: At least two means differ.
  3. H0: At least two means are the same. H1: The means of the levels of factor B are all different.
  4. H0: At least two means differ. H1: The means of the levels of factor B are equal.
