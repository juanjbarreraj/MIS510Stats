Exercise 06-039 (Joint, Marginal, and Conditional Probability)

An analysis of fired or laid-off workers, their age, and the reasons for their departure produced the following table of joint probabilities.

Reason for Job Loss Age Category
20–24 25–54 55–64 65 and Older
Plant or company closed or moved 0.015 0.320 0.089 0.029
Insufficient work 0.014 0.180 0.034 0.011
Position or shift abolished 0.006 0.214 0.071 0.016

(a) What is the probability that a 25- to 54-year-old employee was laid off or fired because of insufficient work?

0.252

(b) What proportion of laid-off or fired workers is age 65 and older?

0.056

(c) What is the probability that a laid-off or fired worker because the plant or company closed is 65 or older?

0.064

Exercise 06-047 Algo (Joint, Marginal, and Conditional Probability)

Credit scorecards are used by financial institutions to help decide to whom loans should be granted. An analysis of the records of one bank produced the following probabilities.

Loan Performance Score
Under 400 400 or More
Fully Repaid 0.17 0.62
Defaulted 0.11 0.10

(a) What proportion of loans are fully repaid?

0.79

(b) What proportion of loans given to scorers of less than 400 fully repay?

0.607

(c) What proportion of loans given to scorers of 400 or more fully repay?

0.8611

(d) Are score and whether the loan is fully repaid independent? Explain.

No because P(Fully Repaid) ≠ P(Fully Repaid | Under 400).

Exercise 06-129 Algo (Bayes’s Law)

The Pap smear is the standard test for cervical cancer. The false-positive rate is 0.639; the false-negative rate is 0.170. Family history and age are factors that must be considered when assigning a probability of cervical cancer. Suppose that, after obtaining a medical history, a physician determines that 4% of women of this patient’s age and with similar family histories have cervical cancer. Round your answers to four decimal places.

Determine the effect a positive Pap smear test have on the probability that the patient has cervical cancer.

0.0513

Determine the effect a negative Pap smear test have on the probability that the patient has cervical cancer.

0.0192
