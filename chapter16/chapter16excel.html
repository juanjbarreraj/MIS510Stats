Excel Activity: Insurance Compensation for Lost Revenues

In July 1990, a rock-and-roll museum opened in one of the cities. The museum was located in a large city block containing a variety of stores. In late July 1992, a fire that started in one of these stores burned the entire block, including the museum. Fortunately, the museum had taken out insurance to cover the cost of rebuilding as well as lost revenues.

The attendance figures for both facilities for December 1991 to October 1995 are listed in columns B (museum) and C (amusement park). During the period when the museum was closed, the data show zero attendance.

Spreadsheet file: Download spreadsheet C16-01-904e3a.xlsx

(a) Use the attendance figures to find a regression equation that predicts museum attendance from amusement park attendance.

Museum attendance = 25.872 + 0.691 × Amusement park attendance

Then use the model to calculate point predictions for the museum's weekly attendance when the museum was closed.

Predicted total attendance: 783487 people

(b) Repeat part (a) using the museum’s argument (after reopening data).

Museum attendance = 398.414 + 0.974 × Amusement park attendance

Use this equation to predict the total attendance lost due to the fire.

Predicted lost attendance: 1158611 people

(c) How should the insurance company determine the amount awarded?

I. Multiply the total projected attendance lost due to the fire by the price of tickets and subtract the museum’s fixed costs.
