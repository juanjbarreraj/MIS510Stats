Exercise 13-012 Algo (Inference About the Difference Between Two Means: Independent Samples)

Use a 10% significance level.

A men’s softball league is experimenting with a yellow softball that is easier to see during night games. One way to judge the effectiveness is to count the number of errors. In a preliminary experiment, the yellow softball was used in 10 games and the traditional white softball was used in another 10 games. The number of errors in each game was recorded and is listed here.

Game Errors
Yellow 61761629310
White 65948129459

Can we infer that there are fewer errors on average when the yellow ball is used? (Let population 1 be yellow balls and population 2 be white balls.)

State the null and alternative hypotheses.

  • H0: μ1 − μ2 = 0
  • H1: μ1 − μ2 < 0

Calculate the test statistic.

-1.51

Use technology to find the p-value.

p-value = 0.0741

Exercise 13-155 Algo (Inference About the Difference Between Two Population Proportions)

Surveys have been widely used by politicians around the world as a way of monitoring the opinions of the electorate. Six months ago, a survey was undertaken to determine the degree of support for a national party leader. Of a sample of 1,200, 57% indicated that they would vote for this politician. This month, another survey of 830 voters revealed that 47% now support the leader. (Let population 1 be 6 months ago and population 2 be now. Use 6 months ago − now.)

(a) Can we infer that the national leader’s popularity has decreased?

  • H0: p1 − p2 = 0
  • H1: p1 − p2 > 0

Test statistic: 4.44

p-value: 0.0000

There is enough evidence to infer that the leader’s popularity has decreased.

(b) Can we infer that the national leader’s popularity has decreased by more than 5%?

  • H0: p1 − p2 = 0.05
  • H1: p1 − p2 > 0.05

Test statistic: 2.23

p-value: 0.0130

There is enough evidence to infer that the leader’s popularity has decreased by more than 5%.

(c) Estimate the decrease in percentage support between now and 6 months ago.

LCL = 0.0560

UCL = 0.1440
