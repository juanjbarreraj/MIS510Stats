Exercise 05-003 (Methods of Collecting Data)

(a) Briefly describe how you might design a study to investigate the relationship between smoking and lung cancer.

  1. Randomly sample smokers and nonsmokers and compute the proportion of each group that has lung cancer.
  2. Randomly sample smokers and compute the proportion of that group that has lung cancer.
  3. Randomly sample smokers who have lung cancer and ask them about their medical history.
  4. Randomly sample nonsmokers and compute the proportion of that group that has lung cancer.

Correct Answer: i

(b) Is your study in part (a) observational or experimental? Explain.

The study in part (a) is observational because experimental data would require the statistics practitioner to randomly assign some people to smoke and others not to smoke.

  1. Yes, because the sampled population consists of the responses only about the professor's course. We can make draw inferences about all courses.
  2. Yes, because the sampled population consists of the responses only about the professor's course. We cannot make draw inferences about all courses.
  3. No, because the sampled population consists of the responses only about the professor's course. We can make draw inferences about all courses.
  4. No, because the sampled population consists of the responses only about the professor's course. We cannot make draw inferences about all courses.

Exercise 05-014 (Sampling Plans)

A telemarketing firm has recorded the households that have purchased one or more of the company’s products. The president of the company would like to know the views of all purchasers but would also like to compare the attitudes of people in the West, South, North, and East. What type of sampling would be most appropriate?

Correct Answer: stratified random

Exercise 05-016 (Sampling Plans)

A statistics practitioner wants to estimate the mean age of children in his city. What type of sampling would be most appropriate?

Correct Answer: cluster

Exercise 05-017 (Sampling and Nonsampling Errors)

(a) Explain the difference between sampling error and nonsampling error.

Sampling error refers to an inaccuracy in a statement about a population that arises because the statement is based only on sample data. Nonsampling error refers to mistakes made in the acquisition of data or due to the sample observations being selected improperly.

(b) Which type of error in part (a) is more serious? Why?

Nonsampling error is more serious because it cannot be diminished by taking a larger sample.

Exercise 05-019 (Sampling and Nonsampling Errors)

Is it possible for a sample to yield better results than a census?

Correct Answer: Yes

Explain.

A census will likely contain significantly more nonsampling errors than a carefully conducted sample survey.
