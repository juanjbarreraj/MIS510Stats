Exercise 17-001 (Estimating the Coefficients and Assessing the Model)

Use a 5% significance level for all tests.

A developer who specializes in summer cottage properties is considering purchasing a large tract of land adjoining a lake. The current owner of the tract has already subdivided the land into separate building lots and has prepared the lots by removing some of the trees. The developer wants to forecast the value of each lot. From previous experience, it is known that the most important factors affecting the price in thousands of dollars of a lot are size in thousands of square feet (x1), number of mature trees (x2), and distance to the lake in feet (x3). From a nearby area, the relevant data for 60 recently sold lots were gathered.

(a) Find the regression equation. (Round your answers to four decimal places.)

ŷ = 51.3912 + 0.6999x1 + 0.6788x2 + 0.3783x3

(b) What is the standard error of estimate? (Round your answer to two decimal places.)

40.24

It is an estimate of the standard deviation of the error variable.

(c) What is the coefficient of determination? (Round your answer to four decimal places.)

0.2425

24.25% of the variation in price is explained by the model.

(d) What is the coefficient of determination, adjusted for degrees of freedom? (Round your answer to four decimal places.)

0.2019

It differs from R2 because it includes an adjustment for the number of independent variables.

The adjusted R2 value suggests the model is a poor fit.

(e) Test the validity of the model.

State the hypotheses.

  1. H0: At least one βi is not equal to zero. H1: β1 = β2 = β3 = 0
  2. H0: β1 = β2 = β3 = 0. H1: At least one βi is not equal to zero
  3. H0: β1 ≠ β2 ≠ β3 ≠ 0. H1: At least one βi is equal to zero
  4. H0: At least one βi is equal to zero. H1: β1 ≠ β2 ≠ β3 ≠ 0

Correct Answer: ii

Test statistic: 5.97

p-value: 0.0013

There is enough evidence to conclude that the model is valid.

(f) Interpret the coefficients.

For each additional thousand square feet, the price on average increases by 0.6999 thousand dollars, provided the other variables remain constant.

For each additional mature tree, the price on average increases by 0.6788 thousand dollars, provided the other variables remain constant.

For each additional foot from the lake, the price on average decreases by 0.3784 thousand dollars, provided the other variables remain constant.

(g) Test to determine whether each of the independent variables is linearly related to the price of the lot in this model.

H0: βi = 0

H1: βi ≠ 0

Independent Variable Test Statistic p-value Linearly Related?
Lot Size 1.25 0.2156 No
Trees 2.96 0.0045 Yes
Distance -1.94 0.0577 No
