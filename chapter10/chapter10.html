Exercise 10-024 Algo (Estimation the Population Mean When the Population Standard Deviation is Known)

This exercise is based on the optional subsection “Estimating the Population Mean Using the Sample Median.” Assume that the population is normal.

(a) Given the following information, determine the 90% confidence interval estimate of the population mean using the sample median.

Sample median = 700, σ = 16, and n = 65.

  • LCL = 695.92
  • UCL = 704.08

(b) Using a sample mean of 700, the 90% confidence interval is 700 ± 3.26. Compare your answer in part (a) to the confidence interval computed with the sample mean. Why is the confidence interval estimate based on the sample median wider than that based on the sample mean?

  1. The variance of the sample mean is larger than the variance of the sample median.
  2. The variance of the sample median is larger than the variance of the sample mean.
  3. The sample median is a biased estimator of the population mean resulting in a smaller variance.
  4. The sample median is a biased estimator of the population mean resulting in a larger variance.

Correct Answer: ii

Exercise 10-038 (Estimation the Population Mean When the Population Standard Deviation is Known)

As part of a project to develop better lawn fertilizers, a research chemist wanted to determine the mean weekly growth rate of Kentucky bluegrass, a common type of grass. A sample of 250 blades of grass was measured, and the amount of growth in 1 week was recorded. Assuming that weekly growth is normally distributed with a standard deviation of 0.01 inch, estimate the 99% confidence interval for the mean weekly growth of Kentucky bluegrass in inches.

  • LCL = 0.493 in
  • UCL = 0.526 in

Briefly describe what the interval estimate tells you about the growth of Kentucky bluegrass.

  1. We estimate that the mean growth rate for this type of grass lies within this interval. We expect this type of estimate is correct 99% of the time.
  2. We estimate that the mean growth rate for this type of grass lies within this interval. We expect this type of estimate is correct 1% of the time.
  3. We estimate that the mean growth rate for this type of grass lies outside this interval. We expect this type of estimate is correct 99% of the time.
  4. We estimate that the mean growth rate for this type of grass lies outside this interval. We expect this type of estimate is correct 1% of the time.

Correct Answer: i
